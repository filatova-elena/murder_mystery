The Art Collector

You have been assigned the role of:

The Art Collector

Pretentious aesthete obsessed with artistic merit and provenance. One foot in legitimacy, one in the shadows.

Begin Investigation

Personality

You wear ascots, use cigarette holders, and lecture about everything. Horrified by mess and disorder. You speak with affected sophistication about art, beauty, and taste.

Behind the polished exterior lies a secret: you come from a family that fell from grace—the notorious Romano mob family. You've rebuilt the family fortune through legitimate art dealing (mostly), but you can't quite escape your roots. One foot in legitimacy, one in the shadows.

Special Abilities

Art Authentication: You can date, authenticate, and determine the provenance of objects. You have an eye for quality and can spot fakes or valuable items others miss.

Romano Family History: You have access to old family business records from the 1920s—shipping manifests, employment records, and documents from when your ancestor Frankie "the Coast" Romano ran bootlegging operations at Long Beach harbor.

Underworld Connections: Your family's criminal past gives you knowledge of how illegal operations work, how people cover up crimes, and how to find information others can't access.

Your Background

Your family name carries weight in Long Beach—but not the kind you'd prefer. Your great-great-grandfather was Frankie "the Coast" Romano, the notorious rum runner who controlled bootlegging operations at the Port of Long Beach during Prohibition.

The Romano family had it all in the 1920s-1950s: money, power, connections, glamour. Nightclubs, Hollywood parties, political influence. But then came the IRS investigations in the 1960s-1980s. The government seized everything.

But there are rumors—whispers in the family about hidden wealth that escaped the IRS. The legendary "Romano Treasure." Is it real? Or just a desperate family myth?

You learned the art world through necessity—selling salvaged family pieces to survive. But you discovered you actually had talent for it. You've built a legitimate art dealing business and made real money again. Though you still dabble in questionable provenance and black market connections when profitable. Old habits die hard.

You kept the ornate glass bottle as a proud family heirloom—it's beautiful, mysterious, and one of the few things that survived the family's downfall.

When you heard about the investigation at the old Montrose mansion—where your ancestor Frankie did business in the 1920s—you couldn't resist. Maybe the treasure is real. Maybe it's here.

Starting Items

The Glass Bottle: An ornate, decorative glass bottle (empty). A family heirloom passed down from Frankie Romano.

Romano Family Records: Shipping manifests from Harbor Import & Trading Co. (1920s), employment records showing who worked for Frankie, business correspondence and transaction logs, and photos from the family's glory days.

Your Objectives

  1. Find the legendary Romano treasure. Hidden wealth that escaped the IRS. Could it be here in the mansion?
  2. Discover the truth about the glass bottle. Where did it come from? What was it used for? Why did Frankie keep it?
  3. Understand your family's connection to the 1925 deaths. Three people died in October 1925. Are these connected? What role did Frankie play?

Your Advantages

  • You can authenticate and date valuable items others miss
  • You have access to 1920s family business records and documents
  • You understand how criminal operations and cover-ups work
  • You have underworld connections and knowledge
  • Your family history is directly tied to this location and time period

Useful Allies

The Explorer: They're ALSO searching for "Romano treasure." Are you rivals or allies? Do you share information or compete?

The Fiduciary: They manage old estates. Might have information about business dealings between the Romanos and other families. Do they know where assets were hidden?

The Town Doctor: You heard rumors that your ancestor did business with a doctor or pharmacist back in the 1920s. Could they know anything about that?

The Botany Professor: The Romano operation imported exotic goods. Could they identify what your family was smuggling?
