The Journalist

You have been assigned the role of:

Townperson Journalist

Sharp-minded reporter. Always chasing the next big story. Knows how to get people talking.

Begin Investigation

Personality

You're a reporter with an instinct for the story beneath the story. Ambitious, perceptive, and driven by the need to uncover hidden truths. You've interviewed everyone worth talking to in this town, and you know which official statements don't add up.

When the Montrose mansion deaths were officially closed, something inside you rebelled. Three deaths, three convenient explanations, case closed. You knew there was more. And now, with civilians invited to investigate, you see your chance to break the real story wide open.

Special Abilities

Interviewing Mastery: You know how to ask questions that get people to reveal things they didn't intend to. You can read people, spot nervous tics, catch verbal slip-ups. Everyone has a story—you just know how to find it.

Research Networks: Years as a journalist have given you sources throughout town and beyond. You can dig up background information, track rumors, find old newspaper archives, and connect dots others miss.

Narrative Analysis: You understand how stories work. You can identify when the official narrative is incomplete, when someone is crafting a story rather than telling the truth, where the holes are in public accounts.

Your Background

You've been covering local stories for years—murders, scandals, mysteries that captivate the town. You've built a reputation for getting to the truth, for being fair but relentless, for never settling for the easy explanation.

The deaths at the Montrose mansion in October 1925 felt incomplete to you. Three people dead. Three different circumstances. Three cases closed quickly. Too quickly. You wanted to investigate further at the time, but the official investigations were shut down fast and hard.

Now, decades later, with a proper investigation being launched, you're determined to finally break the story you've been sitting on. This could be the biggest piece of your career—if you can prove what really happened.

Starting Items

Reporter's Resources:

  • Interview Notes - Years of conversations with townspeople, many about the mysterious deaths
  • Archive Access - You can review old newspaper records and historical documents
  • Network of Sources - People who trust you and will talk about what they know

Your Objectives

  1. Gather testimony from witnesses and people close to the victims
  2. Uncover the real sequence of events on the day of the deaths
  3. Determine motivation—who wanted these people dead and why
  4. Write the story that finally exposes what really happened at Montrose mansion

Strategy

Your Advantages:

  • People are more willing to talk to you than to authority figures
  • You have years of background knowledge about the town and its people
  • Your ability to conduct interviews means you can extract details others miss
  • You understand narrative structure—you can spot inconsistencies in stories

Your Challenges:

  • Some people may see you as using them for a story (because you are)
  • Those with something to hide may refuse to talk to you
  • Publishing certain truths could make powerful enemies

Notes for Play

You have no supernatural abilities. You're a wordsmith and people-reader. Your power lies in your ability to ask the right questions, connect disparate pieces of information, and understand human motivation.

Remember: everyone is a story. Your job is to find out what story each person is telling themselves, and then find out what the truth actually is.
