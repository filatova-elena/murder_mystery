The Fiduciary

You have been assigned the role of:

The Fiduciary

Pedantic record-keeper obsessed with documentation. You hold all the secrets in your files.

Begin Investigation

Personality

Pedantic, detail-oriented record-keeper obsessed with proper documentation and estate management. You know where all the records are, though you haven't always read them thoroughly. Usually found in the Fiduciary's Office, surrounded by files and ledgers.

Your Secret Hobby: You collect poisonous spiders and snakes. You're an expert in toxicology, though few people know this about you.

Special Abilities

Estate Records Expert: When you scan legal or financial documents, you see detailed information others miss. You can interpret contracts, property records, and follow money trails.

Toxicology Knowledge: Your secret hobby gives you expertise in poisons. You can identify toxic substances, understand symptoms, and determine methods of poisoning.

Information Gatekeeper: You control access to official records. You can choose when to share information with others.

Your Background

You work for a generational wealth management firm that has managed the estates of the Montrose, Crane, and Whitmore families for decades. These properties have been a professional frustration—the haunted mansion has been an eyesore and wasted asset for generations.

While researching the Montrose estate, you traced the lineage to find the Heiress and contacted her about the unclaimed property. You hoped she'd take possession and finally do something with it.

During your research, you discovered records about the Crane family name change—they became the Sinclairs in the 1960s, apparently for political reasons. You found a box of old papers connecting Dr. Thaddeus Crane to a brother named Sebastian Crane. You've never heard of Sebastian Crane, and you've been managing the estates for years! You sent this box to Dr. Sinclair, curious about their reaction.

Now you've noticed some disturbing inconsistencies in death records from 1925, and you're determined to finally understand what happened. You've heard rumors about the ghosts haunting the mansion, and you can't shake off a strange feeling.

Your Objectives

  1. Discover who paid the Mortician. Follow the money—who bribed the Mortician to cover something up in 1925?
  2. Determine the true heir to the Montrose estate. The Heiress is a distant relative, but is she the true heir?
  3. Who was Sebastian Crane? The records are incomplete and mysterious—as if someone tried to erase him from existence completely.

Your Strategy

You control access to official records and can decide how to use this power:

  • Share documents openly or keep them to yourself
  • Read everything thoroughly or only what's necessary for your job
  • Reveal information freely or strategically withhold it
  • Collaborate with others or investigate independently

The choice is yours: Will you be the helpful guide or the secretive gatekeeper?

Your Advantages

  • You hold all the estate records and financial documents
  • You understand how to follow money trails and read contracts
  • You have expertise in toxicology through your hobby
  • You know about the Crane→Sinclair name change
  • You control when and what information is shared with others

Key Relationships

The Heiress: You found her and gave her the Montrose mansion. But is she really the true heir? Or is there someone else?

Dr. Sinclair (Doctor): You sent them the mysterious box about Sebastian Crane. How will they react? What do they know?

The Mortician: Payment records show someone paid them in October 1925. Were they part of a cover-up?

The Art Collector: Their family records might connect to the Crane family business dealings. Could the Romanos and Cranes have worked together?
