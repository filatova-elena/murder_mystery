A Local Resident

Personality

You're an ordinary resident of Long Beach—someone who's been around long enough to know the town's dark history, the scandals, the whispers that echo through the streets. You're not wealthy or famous, not a specialist or professional, just someone who's lived here and listened.

You know people. You know stories. You know what the townsfolk say about the Montroses, the Romanos, the tragedies that have touched this place.

Your Background

You're a lifelong resident of Long Beach. You've watched the town grow, watched families rise and fall, seen scandals that were quietly buried. You go to the same shops, the same cafés, attend the same churches as everyone else. You hear the gossip. You know the rumors.

When you heard about the investigation into the old Montrose mansion mysteries, you were curious—like everyone. But unlike the official investigators, you know the town's informal history. The stories people tell when they think no one's listening.

You don't have special abilities or secrets of your own. But you have something valuable: you know what people say. And sometimes, what people say is closer to the truth than official records.

Your Objectives

  1. Share what you know with the other investigators
  2. Separate town gossip from actual facts
  3. Discover which rumors have truth behind them
  4. Help others understand the town's history and context

Your Advantages

  • You know the town and its people intimately
  • You have access to rumors and local gossip that official channels might miss
  • People are comfortable talking to you—you're one of them
  • You can verify which stories are consistent across the community
  • You understand the social dynamics and family histories

Your Challenges

  • You don't have special access or abilities
  • You weren't part of the inner circle of wealthy families
  • Much of what you know is hearsay and gossip, not hard evidence
  • You must figure out what's true and what's just town legend
