Prologue: The Inheritance

The letter arrived on a Tuesday morning, embossed with the seal of a generational wealth management firm.

Miss Montrose,

"We are writing to inform you that you are the heir to the Montrose family estate located at 266 Kennebec Avenue, Long Beach, California. The property has been held in trust for several decades, and our records indicate you are the closest living relative with a legal claim..."

The Heiress—tired of her overbearing family, hungry for independence—saw opportunity. A mansion of her own? In Long Beach? How glamorous. How perfect.

The Fiduciary who tracked her down was thorough, pedantic, obsessed with proper documentation. They'd been managing the Montrose, Crane, and Whitmore estates for years. The mansion had been an eyesore, a wasted asset, abandoned for decades.

So the Heiress moved in, expecting elegance and independence.

She discovered, instead, that she was not alone.

The Three Ghosts

The Lonely Ghost - Alice Catherine Whitmore

Age at death: 28 years old
Date of death: October 11, 1925
Official cause: Accidental fall down the stairs
Actual cause: Murdered by blunt force trauma

Alice appears in a blue dress, pacing the stairs, searching for her best friend Cordelia. She was psychic—truly gifted with visions and premonitions. What others dismissed as hysteria was real sensitivity to hidden truths.

She was in love with Dr. Thaddeus Crane, a forbidden affair that had to be kept secret. When he confessed his crimes to her, she threatened to expose him. He killed her to silence her.

Her spirit is lonely because she lost her best friend, lost her secret lover, lost her own life, and spent 100 years unable to tell anyone what she knew.

What she knows: She knows that Thaddeus Crane poisoned Sebastian's elixir with foxglove and that it killed Cordelia and Sebastian. But she will not give away that secret easily. She knows that someone murdered her - she felt a shadowy figure approaching, then something hit her head and she was dead.

What she doesn't know: She doesn't know that Thaddeus Crane was the one who murdered her.

The Cursed Alchemist - Sebastian Montgomery Crane

Age at death: 32 years old
Date of death: October 12-13, 1925
Official cause: Poisoning, suspected homicide
Actual cause: Accidentally poisoned by his own formula (which his brother had sabotaged)

Sebastian was a talented apothecary and bootlegger known as "The Alchemist" in Frankie Romano's organization. He created an "Elixir of Eternal Love"—a harmless romantic potion designed to strengthen his bond with his fiancée Cordelia.

Unknown to him, his brother Dr. Thaddeus secretly corrupted the formula with deadly foxglove. When Sebastian tested his own creation, he died within days.

He died never knowing he was innocent—never knowing his brother had sabotaged his work. His spirit is cursed because he believes his own incompetence destroyed everything he loved.

What he knows: He knows that his elixir was making Cordelia sick, but ingredients were harmless or so he thought. He knows that the same elixir killed him as well.

What he doesn't know: It was his own brother who corrupted the elixir with foxglove and killed him and his fiancée.

The Heartbroken Spirit - Cordelia Rose Montrose

Age at death: 26 years old
Date of death: October 18, 1925
Official cause: Heart failure, natural causes
Actual cause: Slow poisoning with foxglove over 44 days

Cordelia appears by the piano, playing a haunting melody, crying for her lost daughter and lost loves. She was once a romantic dreamer who believed in destiny.

Her first love, Thomas Whitmore (Alice's brother), was a sailor who left her pregnant. The Montrose family forced her to give the baby away in secret. When Sebastian offered devotion and stability, she chose him.

She discovered Dr. Thaddeus's affair with Alice and threatened to expose him. In response, he poisoned Sebastian's harmless elixir. Sebastian unknowingly administered the poison to Cordelia daily for six weeks.

In her final days, sick and confused, she learned that both her best friend and fiancé were dead. All she could think of was finding her daughter, Eleanor.

She died never knowing the truth.

What she knows: She knows Thaddeus Crane was having and affair with Alice Whitmore and that she threatened him. She was getting sicker for the past month. News of Sebastian's and Alice's deaths recently arrived.

What she doesn't know: She doesn't know that Thaddeus Crane was the one who murdered her, Sebastian and Alice. She doesn't even know anything about the poison or the elixir.

The Murder: What Really Happened

The Secret Affair (1924-1925)

Dr. Thaddeus Crane, the respected town physician, was conducting an illicit affair with his patient: Alice Whitmore. It violated every ethical boundary, but Alice was extraordinary—psychic, brilliant, and she saw him truly.

They kept their relationship hidden. Secret meetings. Coded correspondence. The thrill of something forbidden.

Then Cordelia Montrose discovered their affair.

The Threat (Late Summer 1925)

Cordelia confronted Dr. Thaddeus with an ultimatum:

"End the affair with Alice immediately, or I will expose you to the medical board and to all of Long Beach society. Your career will be destroyed."

Dr. Thaddeus realized he had only one way out: eliminate Cordelia.

The Murder Weapon: A Poisoned Elixir

Dr. Thaddeus knew his younger brother Sebastian was working on an "Elixir of Eternal Love." He examined Sebastian's formula—it was harmless. Foolish and romantic, but not dangerous.

Thaddeus saw an opportunity. He secretly added concentrated foxglove extract to Sebastian's prepared elixir, making it deadly.

September 4, 1925 - The Jupiter-Venus Conjunction

6:14 AM - Sebastian administers the first dose of what he believes is his perfected elixir to Cordelia during the rare Jupiter-Venus conjunction. He has no idea his brother corrupted the formula with poison.

September 4 - October 6, 1925 - The Slow Death

Every morning, Sebastian adds the poisoned elixir to Cordelia's tea. She begins showing symptoms: weakness, nausea, confusion, cardiac irregularities. The poison accumulates slowly.

No one suspects poisoning. Alice senses something is terribly wrong but doesn't understand what yet.

October 7, 1925 - Sebastian's Discovery

After a month of doses, Sebastian grows concerned. Cordelia isn't developing "unwavering devotion"—she's getting sicker. Confused and worried, Sebastian does what any arrogant alchemist would do: he tests it on himself.

He drinks a concentrated dose directly from the bottle. The foxglove-laced elixir hits him immediately. Within hours, he's violently ill.

October 7-11, 1925 - The Desperate Antidote Search

Sebastian is dying. Dr. Thaddeus realizes with horror: He's murdered his own brother. Sebastian was never supposed to taste the elixir!

Thaddeus frantically searches for an antidote—consulting experts, researching treatments, purchasing chemicals. This research leaves a trail of evidence. But foxglove is unforgiving. He can't save Sebastian.

October 10, 1925 (Evening) - The Confession

Sebastian is dying. Thaddeus is wracked with guilt. He goes to the one person he trusts: Alice Whitmore, his secret lover.

He tells her everything: how Cordelia threatened to expose their affair, how he poisoned the elixir to kill her, how Sebastian unknowingly administered it, how Sebastian accidentally poisoned himself.

Thaddeus is desperate for absolution. But Alice is horrified.

October 11, 1925 - Alice's Ultimatum & Murder

Alice urges Thaddeus to confess. Maybe it's not too late. Maybe they can still save Cordelia. But Thaddeus realizes Alice won't back down.

Desperate and cornered, Dr. Thaddeus murders Alice Whitmore to silence her.

Location: The mansion stairs
Weapon: A heavy silver candle holder
Method: Blunt force trauma to the head

He stages it to look like an accidental fall. People will believe it—Alice was "unstable," she had "episodes." A tragic accident.

October 12-13, 1925 - Sebastian Dies

Sebastian Crane succumbs to foxglove poisoning. He dies thinking his own formula failed, that his "Great Work" was flawed.

He never knows the truth: his brother corrupted his harmless elixir. He was innocent all along.

Official cause: Poisoning, suspected homicide. Everyone believes the mob (Frankie Romano) or his criminal dealings caught up with him.

October 18, 1925 - Cordelia's Death

Six weeks after the first poisoned dose, Cordelia Montrose dies. Her body, weakened by 44 days of accumulated foxglove, gives out. Cardiac arrest. Multiple organ failure.

Official cause: Heart failure, natural causes. Rumors spread that she died of a broken heart, devastated by Sebastian's death.

The Cover-Up

Dr. Thaddeus Crane signed all three death certificates as the attending physician. He sends $500 anonymously to Silas Blackwell, the mortician, to keep quiet about discrepancies.

On October 25, 1925, he seals Sebastian's laboratory and hides all evidence—notebooks, formulas, botanical specimens—in the abandoned garage.

In his diary, he writes:

"I could not destroy it all. Perhaps someday someone should know. But not now. Not while I live."

He died in 1952, taking his terrible secret to the grave.

The Competing Narratives: Lies & Truth

SEBASTIAN'S DEATH

❌ LIE #1: Frankie Romano Killed Sebastian

The Story: Sebastian tried to leave Frankie's criminal operation. Everyone heard their violent dock argument. Frankie murdered Sebastian as a warning—you don't walk away from the Romano family.

Evidence: Public dock argument, dock workers' testimony, Sebastian's diary ("Frankie won't let me leave"), Romano violence history, poison method is mob signature.

❌ LIE #2: Montrose Family Killed Sebastian

The Story: The powerful Montrose family had Sebastian eliminated. A pharmacist with criminal connections would destroy their reputation.

Evidence: Montrose ruthlessness (see: Baker's family arson), private investigator reports, large expenditures in October 1925.

❌ LIE #3: Elias Monroe (The Dressmaker) Killed Sebastian

The Story: The rival dressmaker, obsessed with Cordelia and jealous of Sebastian, poisoned him to eliminate competition.

Evidence: Elias's desperate love letters, constant warnings against marriage, documented obsession, access to mansion.

✅ TRUTH: Dr. Thaddeus Accidentally Poisoned Sebastian

Reality: Sebastian died from testing his own Elixir of Eternal Love—which his brother Dr. Thaddeus secretly sabotaged with foxglove. Sebastian was innocent, making a harmless romantic potion. He died never knowing his brother corrupted his work.

Evidence: Sebastian's notebooks showing HARMLESS formula, Dr. Thaddeus's diary confession, antidote research trail (Oct 7-11), Professor Hartley's notes showing non-lethal botanicals, medical analysis showing concentrated foxglove, timeline of Sebastian's death after testing, poison residue in bottle.

ALICE'S DEATH

❌ LIE #1: Alice Fell Down the Stairs

The Story: Tragic accident. Alice was "unstable," possibly drunk or delirious. She fell or took her own life.

❌ LIE #2: Sebastian Killed Alice

The Story: Alice discovered Sebastian was poisoning Cordelia. She confronted him, he killed her, then died from guilt or mob retaliation.

✅ TRUTH: Dr. Thaddeus Murdered Alice

Reality: After confessing his crimes, Alice threatened to expose him. Dr. Thaddeus killed her with a silver candle holder and staged an accidental fall.

Evidence: Injury pattern inconsistent with fall, medical records ruling out suicide, silver candle holder (murder weapon), Dr. Thaddeus's diary confession, timeline (Alice died same night she threatened exposure), Alice's psychic notes about dangerous secrets, mortician's private notes documenting discrepancies.

CORDELIA'S DEATH

❌ LIE #1: Cordelia Died of a Broken Heart

The Story: When Sebastian died, Cordelia couldn't bear it. Already fragile from her pregnancy scandal, she wasted away from grief. Natural causes.

❌ LIE #2: Sebastian Poisoned Cordelia

The Story: Sebastian deliberately poisoned his fiancée with his "Elixir of Eternal Love" because he was jealous and obsessive.

✅ TRUTH: Dr. Thaddeus Poisoned Cordelia

Reality: Dr. Thaddeus sabotaged Sebastian's harmless elixir by adding concentrated foxglove, intending to kill Cordelia to prevent her from exposing his affair. Sebastian unknowingly administered the poison daily from September 4 to October 6. Cordelia died October 18 from accumulated toxin damage.

Evidence: Dr. Thaddeus's confession, medical analysis showing cumulative foxglove poisoning over 6 weeks, Sebastian's original harmless formula vs. corrupted version, Cordelia's diary showing she threatened Thaddeus, timeline (first dose Sept 4, death Oct 18 = 44 days).

Key Evidence

Sebastian's Elixir Formula

Original Formula (HARMLESS):

  • Damiana, Valerian Root, Mandrake Root, Rose Otto - Traditional aphrodisiacs and calming agents
  • Potassium Bromide, Calcium Lactate, Iron Citrate - Medical compounds (safe in small doses)
  • Vanilla, Cherry Syrup, Honey - Flavorings
  • Grain Alcohol 95% - Base/preservative

Dr. Thaddeus's Addition (DEADLY):

  • Foxglove (Digitalis purpurea): CONCENTRATED EXTRACT ADDED
  • Cumulative cardiac poison causing cardiac arrest, organ failure, death
  • Symptoms: weakness, nausea, confusion, vision problems, cardiac distress

Physical Evidence

  • Glass Bottle (Art Collector): Contains poison residue, proves elixir existed
  • Brass Candle Holder (Mansion): Murder weapon used to kill Alice
  • Pocket Watch (Clockmaker): Engraved with Sept 4, 1925 and conjunction symbols
  • Wedding Dress (Dressmaker): Unworn, contains Cordelia's hair, made by Elias Monroe
  • Eleanor's Diary (Baker): Proves the Baker's true identity as heir

Documentary Evidence

  • Pre-nuptial Agreement: Reveals Sebastian-Cordelia engagement (Aug 15, 1925)
  • Death Certificates: All signed by Dr. Thaddeus, contain falsified information
  • Payment Records: $500 to mortician Silas Blackwell (Oct 1925)
  • Dr. Thaddeus's Diary: Confesses to all three murders and cover-up
  • Sebastian's Notebooks: Show harmless original formula
  • Antidote Research Notes: Oct 7-11, Thaddeus trying to save Sebastian
  • Professor Hartley's Notes: Sebastian consulted about non-lethal botanicals
  • Mortician's Private Notes: Document discrepancies in all three deaths
