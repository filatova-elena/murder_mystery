Clue System Reference

Internal Documentation - Game Dynamics & Character Access

This page documents the complete clue system for "The Lost Souls of Kennebec Avenue." It serves as a master reference for how information is distributed among characters and how the investigation mechanics work.

Clue Types Overview

The game uses seven distinct clue categories, each revealing different layers of truth based on character expertise and access levels.

1. Visions (Ghost QR Codes)

Spectral manifestations accessible through QR codes placed at specific locations around the house. Different characters perceive different levels of detail.

Full (All Ghosts)

  • Psychic Medium
  • Grandmother

Full (Specific Ghosts)

  • Cordelia: Baker, Dressmaker
  • Alice: Explorer

Partial (Vague Hints)

  • Baker, Dressmaker (for non-Cordelia)
  • Explorer (for non-Alice)

Mechanical (Static Interference)

  • Clockmaker (all ghosts - timing clues)
Mechanic: When others attempt to scan, they receive: "You sense a presence but cannot communicate with it."

Ghost Locations & Access:

Alice's Ghost (Stairs/Laboratory)

Full: Psychic Medium, Grandmother, Explorer - Complete vision of confrontation with Sebastian, the poisoning
Partial: Baker, Dressmaker, Clockmaker - Fragmented emotions: "poison... Cordelia... he killed me..." or timing hints

Cordelia's Ghost (Bedroom/Dining Room)

Full: Psychic Medium, Grandmother, Baker, Dressmaker - Complete vision of slow poisoning, morning tea ritual
Partial: Explorer, Clockmaker - Explorer gets fragmented emotions; Clockmaker gets timing hints

Sebastian's Ghost (Laboratory/Garage)

Full: Psychic Medium, Grandmother - His obsession, making the elixir, the conjunction, panic
Partial: Baker, Dressmaker, Explorer, Clockmaker - Dark presence and guilt; vague hints specific to their expertise

2. Rumors (Physical Printed Cards)

Hearsay and gossip spread throughout the town. Only ⅔ of rumors are true. Characters with full access collect rumors; all others must share when asked.

Full Access (Collect Rumors)

  • Heiress
  • Influencer

Can ask others to share rumors; others must give up at least one rumor card

Partial Access (Must Share)

  • Everyone else

Must give up at least one rumor card when interacting with collectors

Key Mechanic: When you interact with Heiress or Influencer, you must give up (surrender) at least one rumor card from your hand. They can ask which rumor you're sharing, but you must share at least one. When two collectors interact with each other, they must exchange at least one card each (both give up one card to the other). Rumors must be verified against facts to determine truth.

Rumor Categories:

TRUE Rumors (Must include some)
  • "I heard Sebastian Crane was involved with bootleggers"
  • "They say Cordelia disappeared for months in early 1925"
  • "The Sullivan bakery fire was suspicious"
  • "Dr. Crane and the mortician were paid to keep quiet"
  • "Alice Whitmore had strange abilities - some called her a witch"
FALSE Rumors (Misdirection)
  • "The Romano family had Sebastian murdered"
  • "Cordelia killed herself from heartbreak"
  • "Alice fell down the stairs while drunk"
  • "The Montrose family was respectable and honest"
PARTIAL Truth Rumors (Misleading)
  • "Sebastian Crane was poisoned" (true but makes it seem like murder)
  • "There was a love triangle involving Cordelia" (true but unexpected participants)
  • "The dressmaker was obsessed with Cordelia" (true but with genuine intent, not creepy)

3. Knowledge (Base Information)

Simple factual statements and base information that characters know about the case, people, and history. Unlike rumors, knowledge is always truthful. Examples: "Heiress knows who Cordelia Montrose is," "Doctor knows about a patient's medical condition," "Baker knows the family that lived here."

How it works: Each character has certain pieces of knowledge based on their background and expertise. This is straightforward information they know from their own experience—not specialized interpretation, just facts.

Example - Knowledge About Cordelia:

Heiress knows: "Cordelia Montrose was my distant ancestor; she lived in this house in 1925 and was engaged but the wedding never happened"
Baker knows: "The Montrose family had a bakery order placed before the bride disappeared"
Doctor knows: "Dr. Thaddeus Crane was a physician in town during that era"

4. Botanical Clues (Plant Specimens & Records)

Items or documents mentioning plant-related evidence. Presented as QR codes attached to physical specimens or botanical records.

Full Access

  • Botany Professor

Complete botanical identification and properties

Partial Access

  • Fiduciary (knows poisons)
  • Explorer (knows shipping)
  • Baker (intuitive knowledge)

Limited botanical information based on expertise

Botanical Evidence Examples:

Pressed Flowers (from Elias to Cordelia)
Botany Professor: "Rosa damascena, Digitalis purpurea (Foxglove - POISONOUS), Valerian officinalis"
Fiduciary: "Recognizes the foxglove as highly toxic"
Baker: "These flowers feel wrong somehow... one of them is dangerous"
Everyone else: "Dried pressed flowers, still fragrant"
Plant Specimens in Jars (at laboratory)
Botany Professor: "Digitalis purpurea, Turnera diffusa, Mandragora officinarum, Valeriana officinalis. This is a poison preparation."
Fiduciary: "These cause cardiac arrest. High toxicity level."
Explorer: "Harbor Import & Trading labels - I recognize this supplier"
Everyone else: "Jars of dried plants and herbs"

5. Medical Clues (Death Records, Autopsy Notes)

Evidence related to causes of death, medical conditions, and disease descriptions. Presented as QR codes on official records and documents.

Full Access

  • Town Doctor

Complete medical analysis and diagnosis

Partial Access

  • Mortician
  • Botany Professor

Limited medical details from their perspective

Medical Evidence Examples:

Death Certificates
Town Doctor: "Alice: Blunt force trauma inconsistent with fall. Sebastian: Acute digitalis poisoning with laceration. Cordelia: Cumulative cardiac glycoside toxicity - slow poisoning."
Mortician: "Injury patterns don't match official cause. Blood evidence suggests violence."
Botany Professor: "Digitalis and cardiac glycosides = plant poisons"
Everyone else: "Official causes: Accidental fall, Homicide-poison, Natural causes-heart failure"
Dr. Thaddeus Crane's Diary (Medical Notes)
Town Doctor: "Full medical context - symptoms, diagnostic reasoning, guilt about cover-up"
Mortician: "Timeline of deaths, his complicity with Silas"
Botany Professor: "Plant-based poison references"
Everyone else: "Emotional diary entries without medical detail"

6. Documents (Legal, Financial, Estate Records)

Birth certificates, death certificates, financial records, pre-nuptial agreements. Physically printed; QR codes reveal additional analytical information.

Full Access

  • Fiduciary

Complete legal and financial analysis, can spot discrepancies

Partial Access

  • Mortician

Can see connections to death records and family involvement

Document Examples:

Pre-Nuptial Agreement
Fiduciary: "Legal analysis: Sebastian Crane was Cordelia's fiancé. Property separation clause. Signed August 15, 1925. Witnessed by Dr. Thaddeus Crane."
Mortician: "Signed by the same Dr. Crane who signed the death certificates"
Everyone else: "Legal agreement between Cordelia Montrose and Sebastian Crane, August 1925"
Payment Records ($500 to Silas the Mortician)
Fiduciary: "Large payment from Montrose Family Trust. Purpose: 'Confidential arrangement.' This is clearly a bribe covering something up."
Mortician: "My ancestor received this payment. He kept quiet about something."
Everyone else: "Payment record from October 1925"
Adoption/Placement Papers
Fiduciary: "Critical finding: Placement papers exist for baby to Sullivan family, but NO finalized adoption decree. This means Eleanor legally remained Cordelia's heir."
Mortician: "Documents are suspiciously incomplete"
Everyone else: "Papers about a baby adoption in 1925"

7. Journals (Personal Diaries & Writings)

Personal diary entries, journal pages, and private writings. These reveal intimate thoughts, confessions, and personal knowledge. Presented as QR codes on journal pages or manuscript sections.

Full Access

  • Doctor (Dr. Thaddeus's diary)
  • Character-specific journals (depends on which journal)

Full confessions and personal thoughts

Partial Access

  • Mortician (sees entries related to deaths)
  • Fiduciary (sees financial/legal implications)

Entries relevant to their expertise

Journal Examples:

Dr. Thaddeus Crane's Diary
Doctor: "Full confession - his own handwriting detailing the poisoning, Alice's threat, her murder, his guilt"
Mortician: "Entries about falsifying death certificates and the bribe payment"
Everyone else: "Personal diary with emotional entries about loss and regret"

8. Artifacts (Physical Objects & Heirlooms)

Decorative items, paintings, furniture, and objects with historical significance. QR codes attached reveal provenance and background details.

Full Access (Provenance Expert)

  • Art Collector

Full dating, valuation, provenance, and criminal connections

Partial Access

  • Explorer (physical search, connections)
  • Heiress (family history)

Limited context based on their knowledge

Explorer: "This came through the Romano smuggling operation at the docks"
Heiress: "Expensive, from the 1920s, belonged to someone wealthy"
Everyone else: "Ornate glass bottle, empty, beautiful craftsmanship"

Art Collector: "Swiss movement, gold case, high-end piece owned by someone wealthy. Engraving done locally in Long Beach."
Clockmaker (Special): "September 4, 1925, 6:14 AM - Jupiter-Venus conjunction. This was significant for alchemical timing."
Explorer: "Symbols relate to navigation and astronomical alignment"
Heiress: "Expensive Montrose-era piece"
Everyone else: "Pocket watch with strange symbols and a date engraved inside"
The Wedding Dress (1920s, Silk & Lace)
Art Collector: "1920s style, high-quality silk and lace, custom-made, never worn. Worth $2000-3000 at auction."
Dressmaker (Special): "Made by my ancestor Elias Monroe for Cordelia. Every stitch perfect. He loved her. Her measurements show she was thin, possibly unwell. Lock of her hair in the hem."

Everyone else: "Beautiful white wedding dress from the 1920s, perfectly preserved"

Game Mechanics & Interaction Flow

When a Player Finds a QR Code:

  1. Player scans QR code with their phone
  2. Website checks their character (from character card scan)
  3. System displays appropriate information level based on character and clue type
  4. Some clues require multiple characters to piece together the full truth

Rumor Card Mechanics:

  1. Rumors are printed on physical cards scattered around or distributed to players
  2. Players keep rumor cards until they interact with Heiress or Influencer
  3. When meeting a collector, players MUST show all their rumor cards
  4. Collectors log rumors and track which are true vs. false
  5. Players can discuss and debate if rumors are truthful based on other evidence

Vision (Ghost QR) Mechanics:

  1. QR codes are placed at locations where ghosts appear (stairs, bedroom, laboratory, etc.)
  2. Only Psychic Medium can scan for full visions
  3. Specific characters (Baker for Cordelia, Explorer for Alice, Dressmaker for Cordelia) get partial visions
  4. Clockmaker gets static interference with timing information
  5. All others receive: "You sense a presence but cannot communicate with it"
  6. Visions provide cryptic but TRUE information that must be interpreted

Information Sharing & Investigation Strategy:

  1. Players must interact with other characters to piece together the complete truth
  2. Information flows through social interaction during the game
  3. Some players have privileged information they can choose when/how to share
  4. Different characters know different parts of the truth based on their expertise
  5. The narrative only fully emerges when characters collaborate and compare findings

Design Notes & Principles

Information Distribution Strategy

  • No single player knows everything: Each character has access to different clue categories, forcing collaboration
  • Overlapping information: Some clues can be accessed by multiple characters, allowing verification and discussion
  • Competing narratives: Rumors provide false and partial truths that must be tested against facts
  • Expert interpretation: The same document means different things to different characters based on their expertise
  • Cryptic layers: Visions provide truth but require interpretation; ghosts speak in emotional, non-literal terms

Character Role Balance

  • Information Collectors: Heiress, Influencer, Psychic Medium gather information from others
  • Specialists: Doctor, Fiduciary, Art Collector, Professor have deep expertise in specific areas
  • Partial Witnesses: Baker, Dressmaker, Explorer, Clockmaker, Mortician have limited but crucial insight
  • Unique Access: Each character type has at least one clue category only they or one other can access
Implementation Note: All QR codes should link to a verification system that checks which character is scanning. The JavaScript backend needs to track:
  • Current player's character (scanned from character card)
  • Which clue/document is being scanned
  • Return appropriate information level
